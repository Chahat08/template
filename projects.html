Multi-Modal Framework for Monitoring Active Fire Locations (AFL) and Precision in Allied Agricultural Activities using Communication TechnologiesMulti-Modal Framework for Monitoring Active Fire Locations (AFL) and Precision in Allied Agricultural Activities using Communication Technologies
| Project

Stubble burning is one of the major problems being faced by citizens of our country and is a precursor to various respiratory disorders like Asthma, skin and eye related diseases, low breathing air quality. It is pre-dominant in the Northern states namely Punjab, Haryana and is one of the major causes of decrease in AQI in NCR.

The project proposes a single system that is capable of performing various tasks involved in detection, surveillance and monitoring of Active Fire Locations. Data captured from satellite will be exploited to identify Active Fire locations. This project also proposes to establish a single Command and Control Center for alert generation, information and online report submission to various stakeholders like Police Network, Administrative and Agricultural Officers, Fire Station, etc.

The project will also bridge the gap between farmers and various agencies involved in best practices for crop residue management by providing a common interactive platform.

Development of 5G based UAV Augmented Intelligent Monitoring and Surveillance System
| Project

In the era of Internet of Things (IoT) powered by 5G technologies, Auto-mobile Industry is headed towards a revolution. This revolution will be powered by the services such as Ultra-Reliable Low Latency Communications (URLLC), Enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) and Massive Machine Type Communication (mMTC).

n Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS), vehicles act as connected entities, and exchange data with each other and with the back-end servers on the mobile network. Many established and new industry players have invested a lot of resources in making truly connected and smart vehicles a reality. These application-based solutions often connect to the central system via the mobile network and transfer data through a central server. These communications are often session based and require a light weight protocol for session establishment and continuity. Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) acts as the base for this kind of communication.

A Deep Learning based model works for the identification and alleviation of DDoS attacks in SIP based networks. The system developed here is scalable and highly available with load balancing and failover addressing capabilities. The datasets used for conducting experiments are created by emulating SIP sessions, generating DDoS attacks, capturing the normal and attack flows, and extracting useful features from the packets.

Performance of the system is assessed in terms of Accuracy, Precision, Recall and F1-Score. The proposed model obtains a significant improvement in the performance than the previously existing state-of-the-art techniques in terms of accuracy and detection rate.
